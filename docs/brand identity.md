# SARGAUX — Brand & Design System

Two events. One system. This document is the canonical brand reference for the wedding website.

---

## Part 1: Parent Brand

### 1.1 Brand Essence

**Positioning**
A celebration with two homes: New York and France. The design system is architectural and restrained — it should feel like a cultural institution, not a wedding suite.

**Tone Attributes**

- Structured
- Urban
- Restrained
- Confident
- Warm (but not sentimental)

**Not**

- Romantic script
- Ornate
- Floral-heavy
- Rustic
- Playful

### 1.2 The Connective Thread

Burnt Amber (#D96A1E) appears in both event identities without exception. It carries three references simultaneously: the cover of the 1970 NYCTA Graphics Standards Manual, the livery of the early TGV trains, and home. This is the single visual element that ties the two events together.

### 1.3 Grid Philosophy

All layouts are built on an **8px base unit**. Every size, margin, padding, and spacing value is a multiple of 8. This is non-negotiable across both events.

- Content max-width: 960px
- Page margins: 64px desktop, 24px mobile
- Column grid: 12 columns, 24px gutters
- Baseline grid: 8px

### 1.4 Shared Typography Rules

- Body typeface: **Neue Haas Grotesk** (or Helvetica Neue) across both events
- Body tracking: 0 (tight, as-designed)
- ALL CAPS headings only — no mixed-case display
- Line height: 1.2 for headings, 1.6 for body
- No italics
- No decorative weights

### 1.5 Shared Motif — The Sun Disc

A perfect geometric circle, flat color, no texture, no gradient. Often cropped by the edge of the composition. Never decorative — it is a structural element and the single graphic motif shared across both event identities. Color varies by event (Burnt Amber for NYC, Prussian Blue for France), but the form is identical.

### 1.6 Shared Design Rules

- No pure black. No pure white. No gradients. No ornamentation.
- No bright red. No pastel tones.
- Horizontal hairline rules divide sections. Never doubled.
- Whitespace is structural, not decorative.
- Every visual element must serve a structural purpose.
- QR codes must use the event's primary text color. No box background, no reversal, minimum 0.25in clear space.

### 1.7 Copy Guidelines

**Approved Language**

- Direct, declarative sentences
- "Together with their families"
- "Invite you to celebrate their marriage"
- "Cocktail Attire"

**Avoid**

- Poetic metaphors
- "Happily ever after" / "To follow" / ceremony language
- Exclamation marks

### 1.8 Print Specifications

Paper:
- Uncoated warm white stock
- Minimum 110lb cover
- Matte finish only

Ink:
- Matte
- No gloss coating

### 1.9 System Integrity Checklist

Every new design element must pass:

- Is it geometric?
- Is it restrained?
- Is it urban?
- Is it free of decoration?
- Does it respect the 8px grid?

If something feels ornamental, remove it.

## Part 2: NYC Event Identity

**October 11, 2026 — Bar Blondeau, New York City**

This identity is grounded in the 1970 NYCTA Graphics Standards Manual by Massimo Vignelli and Bob Noorda. The reference is intentional and precise, but never on the nose.

### 2.1 Typography

Typeface: **Neue Haas Grotesk** — no alternative display face.

Type scale derived from the NYCTA Standards Manual signage hierarchy, all values multiples of 8px:

| Role | Size | Tracking | Weight |
|------|------|----------|--------|
| Hero (H1) | 64px | 0.08em | Medium |
| Section header (H2) | 32px | 0.08em | Medium |
| Subhead (H3) | 20px | 0.05em | Medium |
| Body | 16px | 0 | Regular |
| Label / caption | 12px | 0.05em | Medium |

All headings: ALL CAPS. Body: sentence case.

### 2.2 Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| Warm Cream | #F1ECE3 | Background |
| Dark Moss | #2F3F36 | Primary text, headings |
| Burnt Amber | #D96A1E | Accent — NYCTA manual cover |
| Espresso Brown | #3A2E26 | Secondary text, borders |

No other colors.

### 2.3 Motifs

Two subtle references to the Vignelli system, used sparingly:

**Station disc** — a small solid circle (8px or 16px) used as a bullet marker for key information: date, venue, time. Drawn in Dark Moss or Burnt Amber. Functional, not decorative — exactly as Vignelli used it on signage.

**Information band** — a full-width hairline rule above and below key content zones, dividing the page into discrete information panels as subway signage panels are divided. Never more than once per section.

**Skyline block** — abstract NYC skyline as a solid silhouette:
- Rectangular geometry only — no curves, no detail
- No windows, no linework
- Dark Moss color
- 25–35% page height when used
- Used in the footer; never as a decorative element elsewhere

### 2.4 Core Identity Lockup

```
NEW YORK
WITH LOVE

10.11.26
```

```
MARGAUX ANCEL AND SAM GROSS
TOGETHER WITH THEIR FAMILIES
INVITE YOU TO CELEBRATE THEIR MARRIAGE
```

## Part 3: France Event Identity

**May 28–30, 2027 — Village de Sully**

This identity is grounded in the golden age of glamorous international travel — SNCF/PLM railway posters, transatlantic ocean liner advertising, and early Air France. The reference era is 1920s–1950s: Cassandre, Broders, the PLM poster tradition.

### 3.1 Typography

Display typeface: **Peignot** (A.M. Cassandre, 1937). Available on Adobe Fonts.

Peignot is the authentic choice — Cassandre designed it at the height of PLM and transatlantic poster culture. Its lowercase letters render as geometric small caps, making it feel entirely uppercase even in mixed-case settings. Period-correct without being costume-y.

Body typeface: **Neue Haas Grotesk** — same as NYC, per parent brand.

| Role | Typeface | Size | Tracking | Weight |
|------|----------|------|----------|--------|
| Hero (H1) | Peignot | 64px | 0.05em | Bold |
| Section header (H2) | Peignot | 32px | 0.05em | Regular |
| Subhead (H3) | Neue Haas Grotesk | 20px | 0.05em | Medium |
| Body | Neue Haas Grotesk | 16px | 0 | Regular |
| Label / caption | Neue Haas Grotesk | 12px | 0.05em | Medium |

All headings: ALL CAPS. Body: sentence case.

### 3.2 Color Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| Warm Cream | #F1ECE3 | Background — same as NYC |
| Prussian Blue | #1B3A6B | Primary text, headings — replaces Dark Moss |
| Burnt Amber | #D96A1E | Accent — TGV livery |
| Dark Moss | #2F3F36 | Secondary text, borders |

The swap from Dark Moss to Prussian Blue as the primary text color is the visual signal of France. It evokes ocean liner hulls, railway night skies, and the deep blues of PLM/SNCF poster printing.

### 3.3 Motifs

**Destination header** — location name set in Peignot, ALL CAPS, with a thin rule above and below. References the destination labelling on PLM and SNCF travel posters. Used once per page as the identifying mark of the France identity.

**Route arc** — a single thin curved line (an arc, roughly 90°, not a full circle) used as a graphic accent. References curved routes on vintage railway maps and the arc of travel from NYC to France. Used at most once per page, never as decoration.

### 3.4 Core Identity Lockup

```
VILLAGE DE SULLY
MAY 28–30, 2027
```
